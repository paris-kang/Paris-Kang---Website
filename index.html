I'm currently doing child psychology due to my goals of becoming a pediatrician. I originally wanted to do my studies in biology because I was told that it was more beneficial and broad for any medical career, in this case it being pediatrics. After doing my research and meeting with career advisors, I was able to settle with psychology since it goes hand in hand with pediatrics. I was able to learn that psychology has various different branches, which includes child psychology. I've taken about two general classes for psychology, yet I was able to learn the things I needed to know as a kickstart to child psychology. Getting a degree in child psychology would be a starting point for me in terms of my career pathway. I plan on applying for internships and possibly shadowing child psychologists soon. After graduating with a BA in psychology, I hope to be in medical school, receive my residency and doctorate degrees, and be a pediatrician within the next 10 years.

Similar to a barista, I used an ice machine to make our ice cream, wiped down the equipment after, refilled toppings, served the ice cream to customers, and prepared to go ice cream orders. I then became a server.
